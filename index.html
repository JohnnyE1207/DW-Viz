The Defensive Winger (DW) Score

A composite score (0–100) measuring how suited a winger is to a wing-back conversion. The DW score uses the same 15 metrics across five categories as the AWB framework, but the category weights are flipped to emphasise defensive contribution — the differentiating axis for winger-to-wing-back recruitment, since attacking output is already a baseline expectation in this pool.

Cosine Similarity to Mayor

Measures how closely a candidate's playing shape matches Mayor's, independent of absolute level. Calculated from 21 standardised (unscaled) metrics — the 15 DW metrics plus 6 additional style-descriptive metrics that don't drive the DW score but matter for role fit.
15 DW metrics (as above) Crosses · Accurate crosses % · Dribbles · Successful dribbles % · Progressive runs · Key passes · xA · Shot assists · Accurate passes to penalty area / 90 · Defensive duels · Defensive duels won % · PAdj Interceptions · Successful defensive actions · Aerial duels · Aerial duels won % 6 additional style features Progressive passes / 90 · Received passes / 90 · Touches in box / 90 · Fouls suffered / 90 · Offensive duels won % · xG / 90
Each candidate receives a score between −1 and +1, where +1 would be an identical profile shape to Mayor. The six extra features (Passes / 90, Offensive duels / 90 were dropped during analysis for correlation > 0.9 with existing features) capture build-up involvement, advanced-zone presence, and shooting tendency — axes the DW composite doesn't measure.

Archetype Clustering

K-means (k = 4) on the same 21-feature space produces four playing-style groupings. Each player's "primary archetype" is the cluster centroid they are closest to in standardised multi-dimensional space. Fit percentages shown in the detail panel use a global distance scale: 100% would be the tightest observed fit in the sample, 0% the loosest.
